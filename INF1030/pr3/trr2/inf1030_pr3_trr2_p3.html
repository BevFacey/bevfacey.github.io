Practice 2

 

In this activity you will be guided to edit a document. The editing directions are supported by quick demonstrations to show you how to make the changes.

  1. Open the file “Letter 1.” You will use this document to demonstrate that you know how to complete the tasks outlined in following the steps.

  2. In the document, set the margins to Top 1", Bottom 1", Left 1.25", and Right 1.25" in the Page Layout tab on the Ribbon.

  3. To see how to adjust the line spacing, go to the Instructional Videos and select “Changing the Line Spacing.”
  1. Select all of your text using Select All (Ctrl + A selects everything on your page). Set your Font to 12 pt., Times New Roman. For a demonstration on how to do so, go to the Instructional Videos and select “Adjusting the Font.”
  1. Align all the text in the document Left for a full-block-style letter. For a demonstration on how to do so, go to the Instructional Videos and select “Adjusting Text Alignment.”
  1. Create appropriate spacing between the letter parts using your Enter key. Click to see an example to compare your work to.
  1. Make good use of the space available on the page by adjusting the spacing between the date and the complimentary closing, if necessary.

  2. Run a spelling and grammar check and proofread the letter. Make any changes you believe would improve the letter.

  3. Print Preview the letter to see how it will look before printing.

  4. Save the adjusted document as “Letter 1” in your INF1030 WP1: Letters folder.

Practice 4

 

In this practice, you will be asked to write your own letter. There are no demonstrations to assist you. Use this as a check to see if you know how to prepare a personal letter. You may go back and check steps if you need assistance.

  1. Open a new, blank Microsoft Word® 2007 Document.

  2. Save the letter as “Letter 3” in your INF1030 WP1: Letters folder.

  3. Write a thank-you letter to someone who has made a difference in your life, inspired you, or has had a positive impact on you.

  4. Format the letter appropriately, including all necessary letter parts, using full-block style and 12 pt. Arial font.

  5. Spell and grammar check as well as proofread the letter to ensure your ideas are communicated clearly. Make any changes that would improve your letter.

  6. Print Preview the letter to see it in its final form.

  7. Save your final document.
