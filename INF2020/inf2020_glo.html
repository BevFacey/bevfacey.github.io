Course Glossary

 

alpha character: an alphabetical letter key on a keyboard

 

alphanumeric keypad: the keypad used for typing text; has keys for all of the letters of the alphabet and punctuation keys

 

anchoring: keeping at least one finger touching or raised slightly above its position in home row so that the fingers do not lose their proper keyboarding position

 

ergonomics: the applied science of equipment design, as for the workplace, intended to maximize productivity by reducing operator fatigue and discomfort

 

home row: eight keys (A, S, D, F, J, K, L, and ;) located in the centre row of the alphanumeric keypad; the base or home from which all other keys are reached

 

keyboarding: a term synonymous with touch typing

 

QWERTY: a keyboard having the standard arrangement of keys on the alphanumeric keypad; the first five letters in the third row

 

service key: any key that does not type a character

 

shift key: one of several service keys that makes uppercase letters and allows characters from the top row to be typed

 

touch typing: typing without having to look at the keys
