Project 4: Final Project

 

Before beginning the final project, make sure that you are confident in demonstrating all of the skills on the Software Skills Assessment Checklist to determine your readiness for final evaluation.

 

In this course, you have learned some important word-processing skills that will give you the competitive edge in the workplace as well as prepare you to become Microsoft Certified. Now it is time to demonstrate your skills by preparing documents for your final assessment. Keep in mind that you may use this final project to begin creating a professional portfolio of your work to show potential employers and post-secondary institutions.

 

What is Required to Complete this Project?

 

In order to complete this project, you will need

  • directions to complete the five final project pieces
  • the Final Assessment Rubric for all parts of the final project

What Do I Already Know?

 

Through the training rooms you have learned the necessary content and software skills to complete the final projects. The point of this project is to determine if you have retained these skills; however, if you become stuck, the training rooms can serve as a reference, as can Microsoft Office Help.

 

What Do I Need to Do?

  • Create a Final Project folder in your INF2050: WP 2 folder.

  • Print and review the Final Assessment Rubric to understand how your work will be evaluated.

  • Complete and save each of the five final projects in your Final Project folder.

  • Self-evaluate your work to ensure that you have met the Final Project Assessment criteria. By using the rubric to help you self-assess your work, you can catch any mistakes or errors prior to turning in your project for evaluation.

  • Discuss with your teacher a method for submitting your final project for evaluation.

  • Complete the Workstation Routines Rubric.

  • Complete the Basic Competencies Rubric.
Time to Work

 

 

Estimated Required Time: 3–4 hours

 

 

Final Project Assessment Rubric

 

Print the Final Project Assessment Rubric and review the criteria.
