Word processing is a basic skill that everyone who uses a computer should possess. You will use word-processing skills in all areas of your life — home, school, and as you enter the workforce. It’s time to extend your word-processing skills beyond the basics and begin mastering the many functions and capabilities of your word-processing software. In this course, you will use software features and functions to easily produce professional-looking documents faster than ever before. Whether you are writing an application letter or preparing an English paper, INF2050: Word Processing 2 will help you develop industry-recognized workplace skills that employers expect those entering the work force to have.

 

What is Required to Complete this Project?

 

In order to be successful, you will need

  • access to a computer with word-processing software (all examples for this course are based on Microsoft Office Word 2007)

  • a place to create an INF2050: WP 2 folder in which to save your work (discuss with your teacher as to where to store this folder; e.g. on a network, in a folder, on a flash drive, on your personal desktop)

What Do I Already Know?

  • You already have basic word-processing software skills.

  • You have prepared personal letters, reports, tables, and documents with graphics.

What Do I Need To Do?

 

This project is composed of six training rooms:

  1. Introduction to Microsoft Word 2007
  2. Letters
  3. Graphics
  4. Reports
  5. Tables
  6. Review and Secure Documents

These training rooms are designed to teach you the basic components of common documents. Training rooms include the following components:

  • Information on when to use and how to produce documents you will use at home, school, and work

  • Demonstrations that will help you learn basic software commands specific to Microsoft Word 2007

  • Practice activities to help you with concepts presented in the training rooms

  • Opportunities to assess your understanding of the concepts presented in the training rooms

Checking Your Work

 

Use the INF2050: Word Processing 2 Software Skills Checklist to evaluate your level of understanding, and check off the skills that you are able to demonstrate as you progress through the training rooms. You must be competent in all software skills presented in the training rooms before beginning your final project. Print or save a copy of the Software Skills Checklist now.

 

You can repeat training rooms and demonstrations as necessary if you have difficulties with the software or if your work differs from the exemplars. You can also refer to Help menus and online references for software training and demonstrations. If you have any problems, your teacher is there to help.
