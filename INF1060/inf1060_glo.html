Course Glossary

 

absolute (or fixed) reference: a reference that does not adjust when a formula is copied to a new location

 

carpal tunnel syndrome: a medical condition in which the median nerve is compressed at the wrist causing pain, weakness, or numbness in the hand or wrist that can radiate up the arm

 

formula: an entry made up of values, cell references, arithmetic operators, or special functions

 

label: a text entry that consists of alphabetic and numeric characters and most symbols

 

This type of entry defaults to align left in the cell.

 

mixed reference: a reference that includes one absolute and one relative reference

 

relative reference: a reference that adjusts when the formula is copied to a new location

 

repetitive strain injury: overuse of the muscles through repeated movements that put stress on certain body parts, such as wrists or elbows

 

soft skill: a person’s personality, social refinement, language skills, or personal and/or work habits that contributes to efficient and successful productivity

 

value: a numeric entry that is part of a calculation; a date or time entry

 

This data can be used in calculations and align to the right.
