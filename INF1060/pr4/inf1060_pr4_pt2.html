Project 4: Final Project

 

Part 2: Personal Expenses

 

Armed with your debit card and a simulated bank account balance, you are to come up with realistic expenses that you might have over the course of three months. How good are you with your money?

 

Your job is to keep personal records of expenses for a period of three months. On the last day of each month, a personal allowance of $500 is deposited into your bank account. For this exercise, you have no other income.

 

Your bank balance on January 1 is $2509.58. You will use your debit card for every transaction. You are not allowed to spend more money than you have in your account at any time. At the end of the third month (March), you must have entered a minimum of 30 transactions. (Try to enter at least 10 transactions for every month.)

 

 

Open the Personal Expenses spreadsheet, and follow the instructions provided.

 

Instructions

 

Step 1: Replace “Your Name” with your actual name.

 

Step 2: Format the dates to the current year.

 

Step 3: Examine the column headings. Change the headings to suit you or insert more columns for additional expenses.

 

Step 4: Merge, center, and format titles.

 

Step 5: Format the cells accordingly (date, currency, etc.).

 

Step 6: Use formulas to calculate the total expenses.

 

Step 7: Use a formula in the column Bank Balance that will subtract the total expenses from the previous balance and add the income. Copy the formula down at least 30 cells.

 

Step 8: Create at least 30 realistic transactions.

 

Step 9: Incorporate a monthly bank service charge of $5.00. These will occur on the last day of each month.

 

Step 10: On March 31, enter “Total” in the Description cell.

 

Step 11: Total each expense column and the Income column.

 

Step 12: Format the table appropriately. You may want to use one of the built-in formats.

 

Step 13: Create a pie chart that depicts your total expenses. Place it below your data. Format your chart appropriately, giving it a legend; explode your highest expense.

 

Step 14: Save your spreadsheet in your INF1060 SS1 folder in the appropriate place.
