Training Room 6: Simple Formulas

Using a spreadsheet to organize and display your data is useful. Using a spreadsheet to perform mathematical operations is essential to companies to set budgets, calculate profit margins, keep inventories, and so on. Personally, you can use a spreadsheet to keep track of your own expenses, calculate interest on purchases, or create a timesheet for work. Microsoft® Excel® has the ability to make these calculations easily and will update your calculated values as you change your data.

 

In this training room you will

  • create and edit simple formulas
  • review the order of operations (BEDMAS)
  • create formulas by pointing and clicking
  • create formulas by selecting ranges
  • copy and move formulas
How to Do It

 

To successfully complete this training room, do the following:

  • Read and watch instructional videos of topics covered in the training room.
  • Use help and online references should you require additional instruction or software assistance.
  • Complete the Time to Practise 6 exercise.

 

You are now ready, once again, to start working in Microsoft® Excel® 2007. Open Training Room 6: Simple Formulas, and follow the instructions, starting with creating and editing simple formulas. Save your Training Room 6 in your INF1060 SS1 folder.
