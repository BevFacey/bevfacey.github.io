Training Room 7: More Formulas

 

Time to Practise 7

 

Instructions

 

In this exercise you will apply all of the knowledge and skills covered in Training Room 7. Read through the following instructions to see what you need to do to complete Time to Practise 7.

 

Complete the following steps in order.

 

Step 1: Open Time to Practise 7.

 

Step 2: In cell F4 of the Time to Practise 7a worksheet, calculate Carina’s total income for the four months. Use the most efficient formula you can; then use the fill handle to calculate the total income of the other three people. Format the cells for currency if needed.

 

Step 3: In cell B9 calculate the total income of the four people in January. Use the Subtotal function in your formula, and ensure cell B7 is included in your formula. Then calculate the total income for the other three months. Format the cells for currency if needed.

 

Step 4: In cell G2 insert 30% as the income tax rate. Then in column G calculate the income tax amount for each person using an absolute cell reference in your formula. Insert a new column heading called “Income Tax” to match the rest of the table.

 

Step 5: Set up the Time to Practise 7a worksheet to display the formulas.

 

Step 6: Open the Time to Practise 7b worksheet, and complete the following:

  • Sort by city from A to Z.
  • Filter out all Calgary locations.
  • Filter out all entries with an income of less than or equal to $22,000.

Step 7: Save the document as Time to Practise 7 in your INF1060 SS1 folder.

 

Step 8: Open your Software Skills Checklist and complete the chart for the skills you developed in this training room.

 

 

Once you are finished and have saved your work, submit your Time to Practise 7 to your teacher for assessment.

 

If needed, revisit Training Room 7 to review any of the skills in which you had difficulties. If you are confident in your skills and abilities, then move on to Training Room 8.
