Project 3: Spreadsheet Skills

 

Training Room 1: Introduction to Spreadsheets

 

© Dmitrijs Dmitrijevs/shutterstock

You may have already worked with spreadsheets at school. Have you seen the Microsoft® Excel® 2007 software? This training room is an introduction to Microsoft® Excel® 2007.

 

If you are new to spreadsheets or have never used version 2007 before, carefully work through this training room. If you already have experience with spreadsheets, much of this information will be review. You may discover alternate ways to perform tasks or learn shortcuts to speed up your work. Feel free to repeat the training rooms until you are comfortable with the skills taught. Once you are comfortable with the skills covered in the training room, proceed to the checkpoint for practice.

 

When you open Microsoft® Excel® 2007, you’ll recognize some familiar features and functions; however, you will notice a user interface that organizes commands into what is referred to as a ribbon. In this course you will be looking at how to effectively use this ribbon so that the acts of manipulating data, as well as setting up and formatting spreadsheets, are less time consuming.

 

In this training room you will

  • learn key features of Excel® 2007
  • use help functions and online references
  • open and save a workbook
  • insert and delete worksheets
  • move, copy, and rename worksheets
  • change the tab window size
  • create hyperlinks
How to Do It

 

This course is unique in that you will be working in Microsoft® Excel® 2007 throughout all of the training rooms. To successfully complete this training room, do the following:

  • Read and watch instructional videos to discover some of the main features of Excel® 2007.
  • Use help and online references whenever you require additional instruction or software assistance.
  • Complete the Time to Practise 1 exercise.
Key Features of Excel® 2007

 

Study the Microsoft® Excel® 2007 screen to see how the user interface has changed and learn about some of the features. Open Key Features of Excel® 2007 to familiarize yourself with some of the features, functions, and buttons in Excel® 2007.

 

 

Go to the Project 3 Instructional Videos in the Toolkit and watch the demonstration “Looking at Microsoft® Office 2007 for the First Time” to see how to use Excel® 2007 screen features.

 

Using Help Functions and Online References

 

Resourceful, adaptable, life-long learners know where to look for information. There are several ways to find information about how to use your spreadsheet software. You can

  • use the built-in and online Help function
  • access online resources and demonstrations through the Internet

 

Go to the Project 3 Instructional Videos in the Toolkit and watch the demonstration “Accessing Help in Excel® 2007.”

 

Microsoft Online can be a wealth of information. Microsoft has developed a variety of how-to resources to support the users of Microsoft products. You can access training, demonstrations, webcasts, podcasts, and information on how to become Microsoft Office Certified. Microsoft Online can be a place where you go to refresh your memory or to learn a new skill.

 

 

Go to the Project 3 Instructional Videos in the Toolkit and watch the demonstration “Accessing Microsoft Online” for more information.

 

From this point on, you will be doing the bulk of your work in training rooms. Each training room is set up in Microsoft® Excel® 2007 as a workbook. Once you have opened the training room, you will see a number of worksheet tabs at the bottom. Complete all readings and instructions contained within the worksheet before moving on to the next worksheet tab. Ensure that you have completed each worksheet tab and that you have saved your training room in your INF1060  SS1 folder before exiting the training room.

 

 

You are now ready to start working in Microsoft® Excel® 2007 to learn its features and use them effectively. Open Training Room 1: Introduction to Spreadsheets, and follow the instructions, starting with the Help features.
