Project 3: Spreadsheet Skills

 

© Kheng Guan Toh/shutterstock

In today's digital world, technology is everywhere. It is used at schools, in homes, and in the workplace. Having a variety of technological skills is an essential part of your skill set. Knowing how spreadsheets work and how to use them is becoming a basic skill that will help you in your personal life, at school, and as you enter the work world. Personally, you should have a sense of money management and budgeting; for school, you need to be able to solve mathematical problems and organize information; and in the workplace, you may need to perform “what if” analyses to make the business more efficient and/or profitable and present your findings to others in the company. It is essential that you have these basic spreadsheet skills.

 

What is required to complete this project?

 

To complete this project, you will need the following:

  • access to a computer with spreadsheet software
    (Note: All examples for this module are based on Microsoft® Office Excel® 2007.)
  • a place to save your work, such as the INF1060 SS1 folder created earlier
    (Note: Discuss this arrangement with your teacher—e.g., network, folder, flash drive, personal desktop.)

What do I already know?

 

You may already be familiar with spreadsheets from other courses you have taken. Can you remember a time where you had to create a simple spreadsheet to solve a math problem? You may also have entered data into a table using spreadsheet software. If you haven't seen a spreadsheet before, don't worry. This course will take you right from opening Microsoft® Excel® 2007 for the very first time through to using formulas and creating charts.

 

What do I need to do?

 

This project is comprised of eight training rooms:

  • Training Room 1: Introduction to Spreadsheets
  • Training Room 2: The Basics of Spreadsheets
  • Training Room 3: Managing Spreadsheets
  • Training Room 4: Formatting Spreadsheets
  • Training Room 5: Layout and Printing
  • Training Room 6: Simple Formulas
  • Training Room 7: More Formulas
  • Training Room 8: Charts

These training rooms are designed to teach you the basic components of Microsoft® Excel® 2007. The training rooms are designed to include the following components:

  • information regarding when to use and how to produce documents for use at home, school, and work

  • demonstrations to learn basic software commands specific to Microsoft® Excel® 2007

  • practice activities to run through the concepts presented in training rooms, which may be found within the training rooms or the Time to Practise exercises associated with each training room

  • opportunities to assess your understanding of the concepts presented in the training room through the completion of Time to Practise exercises
