Your slide show is complete with all the bells and whistles, but before you publish or present your masterpiece, it is important to find someone who will give you constructive feedback on your presentation. Collaborating with a peer gives you a fresh pair of eyes to help with the final edits so your presentation is flawless.

 

When your edits are complete, you are ready to present or publish your presentation. But where is your audience located? Will you need to set up screens, projectors, or perhaps additional monitors for a live presentation? Will you package your presentation on a CD for easy transportation and distribution? Or will you perhaps publish your presentation as a web page?

 

What will I learn in Training Room 5?

 

In Training Room 5 you will learn how to collaborate on and deliver a presentation. Specifically, you will

  • deliver a slide show with rehearsed timings
  • review and proof your presentations and collaborate using comments
  • secure and share presentations
  • prepare printed materials
  • package and distribute a presentation

The estimated completion time for Training Room 5 and Time to Practise 5 is three hours.

 

How can I improve my learning?

 

To really grasp what you are learning, you can

  • read and watch demonstrations in Microsoft PowerPoint 2007 to discover how to work with visual content in a presentation

  • use the Office Online Help and How-to website should you require additional instruction or software assistance

  • complete all practice activities to learn the software
